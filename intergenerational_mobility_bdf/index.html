# Project

Context :

  • Measurement of intergenerational social mobility is very important.
  • Requirements on data to measure social mobility are very high.
  • There does not exist income mobility indexes for France based on both parent's and children income.
## Goal: 1. Provide measures of intergenerational mobility for France based on parents'/children's taxable income. 1. Build on these mobility indexes to perform more advanced analysis based on geographical analysis.
## State of the Art
1. Data were for long not available to analyze intergenerational income mobility. 1. Recent improvements in access to administrative data provide studies measuring precisely intergenerational income mobility.([Bratberg et al., 2005](); [Chetty et al., 2014](); [Corak, 2019](); [Acciari et al., 2019]()). 1. Some of these studies use varation between localities to identify factors linked with internerational income mobility.
## What about France?
* Studies on equality of opportunities (Lefranc et al., 2009), or on income mobility (Poncelet et al., 2016) exists. * Some using geographic variation (Dherbécourt, 2015; Kenedi and Dherbécourt, 2017). None of these use linked data on parent's on children's income.
## Data and Methodology
## Data We use the Échantillon Démographique Permanent (EDP). * A blend of administrative data and surveys. * Contains income tax return data for a large sample of individual since 2011. * Allows following over time individuals born at specific dates.
## Dataset We use the Échantillon Démographique Permanent (EDP). * A blend of administrative data and surveys. * Contains income tax return data for a large sample of individual since 2011. * Allows following over time individuals born at specific dates. * The sample size is approximately 15,000 households per year.
## Measuring parents' and children's income. 1. We observe parent's income while their tax-dependent children, aged 16-18 years old, live with them. 1. We measure children's income five years later (aged 21-23 years old).
## Intergenerational Mobility Measures IGE: $$IGE = Cor(log \; y_i , log \; y_i^p) \frac{SD(log \; y_i)}{SD(log \; y_i^p)}$$ Rank-Rank Slope: $$R_i = \alpha + \beta R_i^p + \epsilon_i$$ Absolute Upward Mobility: $$\mathbb{E}[R_i | R_i^p < 50] $$ Q1-Q5 (American Dream): $$\mathbb{E}[Q = 5 |Q^p = 1] $$
### (Preliminary) Results
## Overall results: * Upward mobility of 40.9% in France (41.57% in the US) * "American Dream" metric of 2.5% (8.22% in the US)
## Further Analysis:
## Mobility and Bankruptcy: * We will use the _Banque de France_ database of bankruptcy cases (MUSES). * Individual bankruptcy cases * 100,000 new observations per year * Rich variables (income and capital of the debtor, age, sociodemographic characteristics, precise location of housing)
## Mobility and Bankruptcy: * We intend to estimate the following model for a locality $i$: $$\boxed{y_i = \beta_0 + \beta X_i + \beta_1 \text{intergenerational mobility}_i + \epsilon_i}$$
## Other projects: * Populist Voting and Mobility. * Distance to University and mobility. * Income Mobility in the Families of Immigrants and Natives ([Abramitzky et al. (2019)](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3476488)).
